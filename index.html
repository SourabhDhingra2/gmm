Extensive Info on Millets from IYM 2023, Department of Agriculture & Farmers Welfare, ICAR, and The G20


Millets are primarily categorized as major millets, including sorghum, pearl millet and finger millet, and minor millets, including foxtail, kodo, barnyard, proso, browntop, and little millet. They are currently cultivated in 131 countries across the globe over an area of 74 million ha. Indian Millets are exported to 70+ countries. More than 59 crore people in Asia and Africa depend on millets as a staple food. In 2018, millets were rebranded as "Nutri Cereals" by the Government of India, recognizing their nutritional potential, and dispensing the earlier nomenclature of "coarse cereals". This led to a renewed interest globally and the United Nations General Assembly announced 2023 as the International Year of Millets, recognizing their benefits towards achieving sustainable development goals.
